Pitching

About Pitching

When comparing pitching between baseball and softball the first noticeable thing is how the windups are significantly different. In baseball it's an overhand throw to pitch and they are sixty feet away from home plate. But, in softball they pitch an underhand throw and are pitching from forty three feet away. Although people think baseball is harder from the looks at it with the distance of everything because it's so much longer/bigger, there is a lot of different aspects that go into it.

Although baseball pitchers reach a higher velocity, with softball being a shorter distance the speeds and reaction times are very comparable. Another difference between the two sports is that baseball players are pitching off of an elevated mound as opposed to softball players from even ground in a circle. The main pitches thrown in softball consist on a fastball, changeup, curveball, and a rise and drop ball. In baseball the most common pitches are a four seam fastball, a two seam fastball, a curveball, a slider, and a changeup.

Another big impact in the difference between pitching in baseball or softball is the size of the ball. Baseballs are white with red seams and softball are yellow with red seams. The size of a baseball is nine inches (in circumference) and a a softball is ten inches (in circumference). With the baseball being smaller it's easier to hold onto and this makes it easier with different grips allowing them to have many types of pitches to throw compared to softball. But pitches like the drop and rise ball in softball are movements baseball players don't see.

Pitching in both sports is also a mentally straining activity. Successful Pitchers must understand what a batter excels at and where they can take advantage of their weaknesses. Additionally, pitchers will begin to switch up the order of pitches they throw the second and third time through the batting order. Pitchers will start the game throwing fastballs first, then off-speed. As the game goes on, pitchers will begin to throw off-speed pitches first, and then fastballs at the end of the at bat to keep the hitters off guard. All of this makes pitching a physically and mentally strenuous activity.
